\section{Research contract and present decision}
The project studies one concrete question: whether failure-directed domain randomization improves
zero-shot transfer of a parameter-shared, high-level 2-v-1 soccer policy from a fast abstract
simulator to an independent Pymunk implementation, relative to equal-budget nominal and uniformly
randomized MAPPO. It is not a general football benchmark, a full-team system, or a physical
sim-to-real claim.

The status after the completed Phase 2 notebook is \textbf{yellow / continue within Phase 2}.
Engineering validity is established and the single-seed result is non-trivial. Failure-directed
MAPPO improves canonical Pymunk success and the broad profile mean, but loses to nominal MAPPO on
the delay--noise grid. Only seed 0 has been trained, and passing is nearly absent. The immediate
work is explanation plus replication; Webots, full-game football, and physical deployment are not
the next step.

\section{Environment and algorithm index}
The field is $9\,\mathrm{m}\times6\,\mathrm{m}$ with a $1.5\,\mathrm{m}$ goal. One macro decision
is repeated for four $0.1\,\mathrm{s}$ low-level steps. The abstract implementation uses explicit
bounded kinematics and collision/ball rules. Pymunk independently uses rigid bodies, five physics
substeps, force/torque velocity tracking, static player containment, ball line crossing, drag,
friction, elasticity, and impulse kicks. Both expose the same 61-dimensional local observation and
seven-action interface.

\section{Perturbation profiles and curriculum}
The 17 non-nominal profiles comprise low, medium, and high action delay; low, medium, and high
observation noise; low, medium, and high communication impairment; weak and strong kicks; low and
high ball friction; slow and fast movement; and combined moderate/severe conditions. Uniform DR selects uniformly from
these profiles outside the 0.10 nominal fraction. Failure-directed DR uses the same candidates but
updates a distribution from a failure-rate exponential moving average.

There are 12 FDR updates (updates 20 through 240) and 204 history rows. At update 240 the conditional
non-nominal distribution spans 0.0359--0.0823, with entropy 2.7946 versus maximum
$\log(17)=2.8332$. High delay is largest (probability 0.0823; smoothed failure 0.997), followed by
low ball friction (0.0790), medium observation noise (0.0771), and the two combined profiles
(0.0732/0.0728). Five evaluation episodes per profile give raw rates only in increments of 0.2;
the update signal is therefore noisy even after smoothing.

\section{Phase 1 provenance retained}
Phase 1 originally exposed a saturated Pymunk baseline, reflective ball walls, player escape,
possession normalization above one, a misaggregated defender minimum, and a weak role heuristic.
Repairs unified terminal classification, added player-only containment, calibrated Pymunk ball
drag and kicks, normalized metrics once per macro step, separated defender/profile aggregations,
paired seeds, and tested role logic. The clean Phase 1 run at commit \texttt{16c94cd} passed the
entry gate: abstract scripted success was 0.03/0.15/0.28 for random/double-chase/role and Pymunk
success was 0.49/0.68/0.66. Fresh nominal IPPO reached 0.87 against its abstract intercept defender
but only 0.34 in Pymunk. Phase 2 was launched to address this genuine transfer failure.

\subsection{Paired conditional tests}
On identical Pymunk transfer seeds, FDR versus nominal has 33 FDR-only successes and 13
nominal-only successes (two-sided exact McNemar $p=0.00453$); versus uniform it has 38 and 12
($p=0.000306$). Across profile episodes the corresponding counts are 278/184
($p=1.42\times10^{-5}$) and 242/147 ($p=1.68\times10^{-6}$). Across grid episodes, however, FDR
loses to nominal 98/162 ($p=8.66\times10^{-5}$) and is not detectably different from uniform at
118/108 ($p=0.549$). These tests condition on the three actors; they do not quantify variability
over training seeds.

Against the clean Phase 1 heuristics on the same 100 nominal Pymunk seeds, FDR scores 0.640 versus
0.490 random, 0.680 double chase, and 0.660 role based. FDR-only/baseline-only discordant counts and
exact $p$-values are 34/19 ($p=0.0534$), 18/22 ($p=0.636$), and 20/22 ($p=0.878$), respectively.
Thus FDR clearly improves over learned MAPPO controls in this pilot but does not beat the deliberate
heuristics.

\section{Comparison/export defect and repair}
The pilot comparison-row collector retained each suite, but its LaTeX exporter and plotter grouped
by only \texttt{method, simulator}. That mixed transfer, profile, robustness, historical Phase 1,
and duplicate-run rows. The defect affected presentation, not the underlying episode files. The
new comparison path carries \texttt{evaluation\_name}, training seed, and source run; aggregates
only within an exact suite; deduplicates manifests; and pairs canonical abstract-intercept and
Pymunk-transfer rows from the same run. It emits raw, suite-level, canonical-gap, and seed-level
replication CSV/JSON files. Automated tests guard both suite isolation and canonical pairing.

\section{Final audit statement}
The current results are promising and internally coherent enough to justify continued focused
work. They are not clean enough to justify a broad robustness claim. The strongest positive result
is FDR's 0.640 canonical Pymunk transfer and 0.521 profile mean; the strongest negative result is
its 0.366 grid score versus nominal's 0.494 and its 0.240--0.260 medium/high delay success. The
next milestone is not ``more football.'' It is a verified delay mechanism, a real cooperation
probe, and replicated evidence under a locked analysis plan.
