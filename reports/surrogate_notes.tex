smoke configuration. Every run stores the resolved YAML, source commit, package versions, device,

\section{Detailed environment mechanics}
The field is $9\,\mathrm{m}\times6\,\mathrm{m}$ with a $1.5\,\mathrm{m}$ goal. A macro decision is
repeated for four $0.1\,\mathrm{s}$ low-level steps. The abstract model limits acceleration,
translation, and turn rate; applies linear ball drag; resolves circular contacts; and delivers kick
velocity only inside range and angle tolerances. The transfer model independently creates Pymunk
bodies, force controllers, collision shapes, walls with goal openings, damping, friction,
elasticity, and impulse kicks over multiple integration substeps.

Observation and action delay queues are bounded. Object misses set confidence to zero without
changing truth. Packet loss preserves the previous delivered teammate action and increments its
age. The intercept defender protects goal center for a distant ball, tracks a delayed predicted
intercept when threatened, and clears only inside control range.

\section{Perturbation-profile definitions}
Named profiles cover low, medium, and high action delay; observation corruption at three levels;
communication impairment at three levels; weak/strong kicks; low/high ball friction; slow/fast
movement response; and combined moderate/severe conditions. Reset samples continuous values inside
each YAML range and records both profile and realization. Family-level ablations filter names rather
than changing simulator code.

\section{Curriculum-update derivation}
For success $q_i$, failure $1-q_i$ enters an EMA
$f_i\leftarrow d f_i+(1-d)(1-q_i)$. Weights $(\epsilon+f_i)^\alpha$ are normalized, mixed with
$\beta/N$, then projected onto the capped simplex. Defaults are $d=0.5$, $\epsilon=0.05$,
$\alpha=1$, $\beta=0.1$, and cap 0.25. Curriculum seeds begin at 20,000 and do not update running
normalizers.

\section{PPO implementation notes}
Rollouts are stored as time, environment, agent, feature arrays. MAPPO has one value and one GAE
advantage per environment transition; actor training broadcasts that advantage, while critic
training does not duplicate it. IPPO flattens local value samples across both agents. Truncations
bootstrap from the final observation but stop the recursive advantage chain across reset. Loss and
gradient finiteness is mandatory; a NaN raises after an emergency checkpoint attempt.

\section{Seed allocation}
Training begins at the experiment seed. Ordinary validation, curriculum validation, abstract final
test, Pymunk transfer, and video scenarios begin at 10,000, 20,000, 30,000, 40,000, and 50,000
respectively. Final runs must record every exact seed and must not reuse model-selection outcomes as
final tests.

\section{Hyperparameter attempts}
Record each deviation from the checked-in defaults, the validation evidence motivating it, and
whether the choice was made before final-test evaluation. The Phase 1 nominal IPPO run used the
checked-in defaults; no post-test hyperparameter selection was performed. Its selected checkpoint
occurred at 1,810,432 environment steps with validation success 0.825 over 40 fixed validation
episodes. The final checkpoint at 2,007,040 steps scored 0.750 on the same-size validation batch,
so the selection rule correctly retained the earlier checkpoint.

\section{Failed runs and debugging notes}
All three restored Phase 1 run records have status \texttt{complete} and no failure exception. A
completed process is not necessarily a valid experiment; the following audit findings block the
Phase 2 launch.
\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Pymunk benchmark saturation.} Random, double-chase, role-based, and nominal-IPPO
success rates are 0.760, 0.730, 0.740, and 0.730. Random and IPPO used the same seeds
40,000--40,099; their paired outcome counts are 58 both-success, 9 both-failure, 15 IPPO-only, and
18 random-only (two-sided exact paired $p=0.728$). The learned actor is therefore not distinguished
from random behavior by the current transfer suite.
\item \textbf{Boundary mismatch.} Abstract baseline out-of-bounds rates are 0.54--0.85, but Pymunk
rates are 0.00--0.01. The abstract simulator terminates a ball beyond the sideline while Pymunk
uses reflective walls. Player bodies can also escape through goal openings; this occurs in the
recorded trajectories. Align or explicitly calibrate these semantics before interpreting a
cross-simulator gap.
\item \textbf{Metric range violation.} Possession is incremented at every physics substep and
divided by macro-action steps. With four repeats it can exceed 1.0 (the observed episode maximum is
3.864). Normalize by executed substeps or count once per macro step, add range assertions, and
regenerate summaries.
\item \textbf{Misnamed minimum.} The standard evaluation has only the nominal profile. Grouping by
profile makes its reported minimum-profile success 0.453, although the minimum across defender
modes is 0.060. Report the defender-mode minimum separately and reserve profile minima for a
profile suite.
\item \textbf{Weak behavioral anchors.} The role-based abstract heuristic succeeds in only 0.060
of episodes and exits out of bounds in 0.85, worse than random success 0.110. The learned policy
makes zero valid pass attempts in 400 final-evaluation episodes and has invalid-action fractions
0.237 on nominal abstract intercept and 0.558 in Pymunk. Inspect action validity and role behavior
before treating goal rate as evidence of cooperative skill use.
\end{enumerate}

The IPPO optimization trace itself is healthy: all recorded scalar diagnostics are finite, mean
training success rises from 0.061 over the first 25 updates to 0.769 over the last 25, and nominal
intercept test success is 0.840 over 100 episodes. Preserve the best checkpoint. Do not spend
accelerator time on another long run until the evaluation defects above are repaired.

\section{Discarded ideas}
Version one intentionally excludes image observations, joint-level locomotion learning, full 5v5
play, self-play, learned opponents, RNNs, ROS, and physical-deployment claims. Each would add an
independent source of variance or infrastructure before the cross-simulator causal comparison is
established.

\section{Webots setup attempts}
Webots is documented but neither scaffolded nor claimed as tested. Log simulator version, official
NAO/OP3 asset provenance, headless command, motion assets, controller trace, and exact failure here
before changing that status.

\section{Known limitations}
Skill adapters encode task knowledge; the defender is scripted; synchronous collection may limit
throughput; feed-forward actors infer delay only through short observation history; Pymunk remains
a simple planar world; and robustness profiles cannot span unmodeled hardware failures. The Phase
1 checkpoint adds observed limitations: opponent-mode generalization is poor (0.060 against
chase-ball versus 0.840 against the trained intercept defender), no passing behavior was observed,
and the current Pymunk protocol is too permissive to measure policy quality.

\section{Phase 2 entry gate}
Phase 2 is a no-go until all of the following are satisfied.
\begin{itemize}
\item Correct possession normalization and defender/profile aggregation, with tests asserting
bounded fractions and the intended grouping.
\item Make abstract and Pymunk boundary/termination choices comparable or document and calibrate a
deliberate difference; keep every player in a valid playable region.
\item Rerun 100 episodes per scripted method in both simulators and confirm that Pymunk outcomes
separate meaningful behavior from random action selection.
\item Re-evaluate the frozen Phase 1 best IPPO checkpoint on the corrected abstract and Pymunk
suites. Retrain only if abstract dynamics change or nominal learning fails to reproduce.
\item Inspect representative successes and failures and confirm that outcome labels match the
rendered trajectories.
\end{itemize}

\section{Future physical-robot deployment plan}
First validate TorchScript observation parity in a supervised simulator. Then integrate a tested
localization and ball/robot perception stack, collision-safe walking and turning, controlled kick
motions, communication timestamps, action watchdogs, emergency stops, and conservative field
tests. A successful deployment requires logged physical trials, not merely controller startup.

\section{Reproducibility checklist}
\begin{itemize}
\item Record clean commit, resolved YAML, packages, device, and all seed ranges.
\item Pass Ruff, the full test suite, both PettingZoo API checks, and the smoke command.
\item Preserve complete checkpoints, normalizers, curriculum history, CSV metrics, and logs.
\item Evaluate frozen checkpoints on fixed abstract and Pymunk seeds without retraining.
\item Aggregate multiple seeds and report variability, tail metrics, and failed runs.
\item Rebuild both reports from restored Drive artifacts in a fresh Colab runtime.
\item Describe Pymunk as sim-to-sim and do not infer physical-robot performance.
\end{itemize}
